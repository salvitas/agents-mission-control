# Reusable Rockets and Launch Economics

**Domain:** science-space
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Research status:** pending_review

## Executive Summary
Reusability continues to pressure launch costs downward, but margin durability depends on refurbishment efficiency, launch cadence, and payload mix. Cost leadership is becoming an operational excellence problem.

## Key Findings
- Frequent launch operations create learning-curve gains that new entrants struggle to match. _[confidence: medium]_
- Insurance, regulatory approvals, and range availability still influence effective cost per mission. _[confidence: medium]_
- Vertical integration can improve reliability but raises capital intensity and concentration risk. _[confidence: medium]_
