# Stablecoin Regulation and Bank Adoption

**Domain:** future-of-finance
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Research status:** pending_review

## Executive Summary
Policy is moving from ambiguity to supervised integration. The strongest near-term shift is banks piloting tokenized deposits and settlement rails rather than replacing core ledgers overnight.

## Key Findings
- US and EU frameworks are converging on reserve quality, redemption rights, and issuer supervision; this reduces legal uncertainty but raises compliance cost. _[confidence: medium]_
- Banks are adopting a two-track model: experiment with stablecoin settlement while preserving traditional deposit franchises. _[confidence: medium]_
- Interoperability with payment messaging (ISO 20022) and sanctions tooling is becoming the gating factor, not token throughput. _[confidence: medium]_

## Contradictions
- **Claim:** Clear rules accelerate adoption | **Counter-signal:** Licensing and prudential requirements can slow smaller issuers and reduce experimentation speed | **Confidence:** high
- **Claim:** Bank involvement de-risks stablecoins | **Counter-signal:** Bank-grade controls improve trust, but concentration risk grows if issuance centralizes | **Confidence:** medium
