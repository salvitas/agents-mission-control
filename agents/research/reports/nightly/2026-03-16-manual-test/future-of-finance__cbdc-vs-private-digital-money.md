# CBDCs vs Private Digital Money

**Domain:** future-of-finance
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Research status:** pending_review

## Executive Summary
The practical trajectory is coexistence, not winner-take-all. CBDCs are advancing in wholesale and programmable public-service use cases, while private digital money keeps moving faster in retail and cross-border experiments.

## Key Findings
- Central banks are prioritizing resilience, privacy design, and offline capability over feature velocity. _[confidence: medium]_
- Private issuers iterate faster but face recurring trust tests around reserves, governance, and jurisdictional exposure. _[confidence: medium]_
- Cross-border pilots highlight that governance alignment matters more than technical architecture alone. _[confidence: medium]_

## Contradictions
- **Claim:** CBDCs will displace private stablecoins | **Counter-signal:** Policy design in several regions points to complementary roles rather than displacement | **Confidence:** medium
- **Claim:** Private money scales because it is innovation-friendly | **Counter-signal:** Without clear safeguards, regulatory pressure can abruptly limit market access | **Confidence:** high
