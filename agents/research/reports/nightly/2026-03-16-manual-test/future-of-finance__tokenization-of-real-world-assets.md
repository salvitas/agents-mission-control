# Tokenization of Real-World Assets (RWA)

**Domain:** future-of-finance
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Research status:** pending_review

## Executive Summary
RWA tokenization is shifting from proof-of-concept to selective production in money-market funds, private credit, and collateral operations. The bottleneck is legal enforceability across jurisdictions, not demand for 24/7 rails.

## Key Findings
- Tokenized treasuries are becoming the flagship product because valuation is transparent and operational risk is comparatively lower. _[confidence: medium]_
- Institutions prioritize transfer-agent integration and compliant custody over public-chain composability narratives. _[confidence: medium]_
- Secondary liquidity remains shallow; distribution and investor onboarding dominate performance outcomes. _[confidence: medium]_

## Contradictions
- **Claim:** Tokenization will democratize access quickly | **Counter-signal:** Many products still require accredited status and gated venues | **Confidence:** high
- **Claim:** On-chain settlement always lowers costs | **Counter-signal:** Operational savings can be offset by legal, custody, and reporting overhead in early phases | **Confidence:** medium
