# Neutral-Atom, Photonic, and Superconducting Convergence

**Domain:** quantum  
**Date:** 2026-03-17

## A) Executive Summary
Hardware modality competition is broadening into a convergence race shaped by software stacks and control systems. Neutral-atom, photonic, and superconducting approaches each show distinct strengths under different workload assumptions. Portfolio strategy should hedge platform risk rather than bet exclusively on one modality.

## D) Contradictions Found
- Each vendor presents its modality as dominant, but benchmark comparability remains poor across architectures.
- Technical roadmaps highlight qubit counts, while users care more about logical fidelity and tooling maturity.
- Government funding narratives suggest winners, yet procurement cycles can lag technical reality by years.

## E) Research Gaps
- Lack of standardized, public incident taxonomies across operators.
- Sparse long-horizon cost-of-ownership datasets.
- Limited independent replication of vendor-reported performance in real workloads.

## F) Recommendations
- Prioritize initiatives that reduce concentration and single-vendor dependency.
- Tie expansion decisions to auditable reliability/SLA evidence, not launch cadence.
- Stage deployments with explicit rollback drills and governance checkpoints.

## G) Bias Safeguards
- Contrarian check 1: Assume reported growth is inflated by incentive programs; recompute without subsidies.
- Contrarian check 2: Assume policy timelines slip by 12 months; stress-test ROI and runway.
- Contrarian check 3: Assume top benchmark claims do not transfer to production; validate on in-house workloads.
