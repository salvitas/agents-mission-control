# Observatory Data Tsunami and AI-Native Astronomy

**Domain:** science-space  
**Date:** 2026-03-17

## A) Executive Summary
Next-generation observatories are creating a data operations challenge as much as a scientific opportunity. AI-assisted pipelines are becoming mandatory to triage events, classify anomalies, and allocate follow-up resources. Institutions with strong compute-governance integration will capture disproportionate scientific output.

## D) Contradictions Found
- Data volume projections are celebrated, but compute and curation budgets are not scaling proportionally.
- Automated discovery improves recall, while false-positive management remains a persistent bottleneck.
- Open-data commitments are expanding, yet practical access still depends on tooling and compute proximity.

## E) Research Gaps
- Lack of standardized, public incident taxonomies across operators.
- Sparse long-horizon cost-of-ownership datasets.
- Limited independent replication of vendor-reported performance in real workloads.

## F) Recommendations
- Prioritize initiatives that reduce concentration and single-vendor dependency.
- Tie expansion decisions to auditable reliability/SLA evidence, not launch cadence.
- Stage deployments with explicit rollback drills and governance checkpoints.
