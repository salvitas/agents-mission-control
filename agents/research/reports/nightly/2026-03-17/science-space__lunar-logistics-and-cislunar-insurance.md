# Lunar Logistics and Cislunar Insurance Economics

**Domain:** science-space  
**Date:** 2026-03-17

## A) Executive Summary
Cislunar activity is moving from mission-centric planning to logistics and risk-pricing economics. Insurance, communications, and payload reliability are becoming as decisive as launch capability. The next investable layer is infrastructure that reduces mission uncertainty across repeated operations.

## D) Contradictions Found
- Mission announcements imply momentum, while actual cadence and payload outcomes remain volatile.
- Public agencies signal long-term commitment, but private financing still hinges on near-term contract visibility.
- Insurance assumptions often rely on sparse actuarial history, increasing model error risk.

## E) Research Gaps
- Lack of standardized, public incident taxonomies across operators.
- Sparse long-horizon cost-of-ownership datasets.
- Limited independent replication of vendor-reported performance in real workloads.

## F) Recommendations
- Prioritize initiatives that reduce concentration and single-vendor dependency.
- Tie expansion decisions to auditable reliability/SLA evidence, not launch cadence.
- Stage deployments with explicit rollback drills and governance checkpoints.
