# Frontier Model Evals Shift from IQ to Cost-Latency-Reliability

**Domain:** ai  
**Date:** 2026-03-17

## A) Executive Summary
Frontier AI competition is transitioning from benchmark one-upmanship to deployment economics. Buyers increasingly evaluate capability per dollar, latency stability, and failure-recovery behavior over headline scores. Durable leaders will be those that combine strong evals with predictable operational performance.

## D) Contradictions Found
- Public benchmarks imply clear rank order, but enterprise evals often flip rankings under cost/latency constraints.
- Provider-reported safety improvements are difficult to compare due to inconsistent taxonomies.
- Open-weight momentum is strong, yet many teams still prefer managed closed models for compliance and support.

## E) Research Gaps
- Lack of standardized, public incident taxonomies across operators.
- Sparse long-horizon cost-of-ownership datasets.
- Limited independent replication of vendor-reported performance in real workloads.

## F) Recommendations
- Prioritize initiatives that reduce concentration and single-vendor dependency.
- Tie expansion decisions to auditable reliability/SLA evidence, not launch cadence.
- Stage deployments with explicit rollback drills and governance checkpoints.

## G) Bias Safeguards
- Contrarian check 1: Assume reported growth is inflated by incentive programs; recompute without subsidies.
- Contrarian check 2: Assume policy timelines slip by 12 months; stress-test ROI and runway.
- Contrarian check 3: Assume top benchmark claims do not transfer to production; validate on in-house workloads.
