# Quantum Error Correction Milestones vs Logical Utility

**Domain:** quantum  
**Date:** 2026-03-17

## A) Executive Summary
Quantum error-correction milestones are significant, but commercial utility still depends on logical qubit quality and runtime economics. The field is crossing important scientific thresholds without yet resolving practical workload advantage. Strategic positioning should separate physics progress from near-term business claims.

## D) Contradictions Found
- Record-breaking experiments show progress, but utility claims often extrapolate beyond demonstrated circuit depth.
- Roadmaps emphasize scaling trajectories, while independent analyses stress fabrication and control-system bottlenecks.
- Investors reward milestone announcements, yet enterprise adoption waits for repeatable problem-specific wins.

## E) Research Gaps
- Lack of standardized, public incident taxonomies across operators.
- Sparse long-horizon cost-of-ownership datasets.
- Limited independent replication of vendor-reported performance in real workloads.

## F) Recommendations
- Prioritize initiatives that reduce concentration and single-vendor dependency.
- Tie expansion decisions to auditable reliability/SLA evidence, not launch cadence.
- Stage deployments with explicit rollback drills and governance checkpoints.

## G) Bias Safeguards
- Contrarian check 1: Assume reported growth is inflated by incentive programs; recompute without subsidies.
- Contrarian check 2: Assume policy timelines slip by 12 months; stress-test ROI and runway.
- Contrarian check 3: Assume top benchmark claims do not transfer to production; validate on in-house workloads.
