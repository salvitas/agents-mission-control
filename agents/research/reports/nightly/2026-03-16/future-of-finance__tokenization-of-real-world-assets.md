# Tokenization of Real-World Assets (RWA)

**Domain:** future of finance  
**Date:** 2026-03-16

## A) Executive Summary
RWA tokenization is shifting from proof-of-concept to selective production in funds, treasuries, and collateral workflows. The strategic question is less about “can assets be tokenized” and more about legal enforceability, transfer restrictions, and secondary liquidity quality. Growth is real but concentrated in short-duration products and permissioned rails.

## C) Key Findings
- Tokenized treasuries have grown rapidly relative to other RWAs, indicating strongest demand for on-chain cash-equivalent yield instruments [RWA.xyz market data, 2025] [Independent/Data] [MEDIUM].
- Regulatory perimeter is clearer for fund-token wrappers than for broad retail asset tokenization in many jurisdictions [ESMA DLT Pilot; FCA sandbox updates, 2023-2025] [Policy] [MEDIUM].
- Institutional pilots show efficiency gains in collateral mobility and settlement windows [DTCC/industry pilot releases, 2024-2025] [Industry/Operator] [MEDIUM].
- Secondary liquidity remains thin; many “on-chain” assets still depend on off-chain transfer agents and gated venues [BIS tokenization analyses, 2023-2024] [Independent/Policy] [HIGH CONFIDENCE].
- [SPECULATION] Cross-chain identity and compliance standards could unlock broader liquidity but interoperability fragmentation may persist [ERC-3643 and institutional standards discussions, 2024-2025] [Technical/Standards] [LOW].

## D) Contradictions Found
- “Tokenization democratizes access” vs “most live products are institutional/permissioned.” Disagreement is due to compliance constraints and distribution strategy.
- “On-chain = instant liquidity” vs “liquidity still market-maker and venue constrained.” Confusion stems from conflating technical transferability with executable depth.

## E) Research Gaps

- - No common reporting standard for true end-to-end settlement latency.
