# CBDCs vs Private Digital Money

**Domain:** future of finance  
**Date:** 2026-03-16

## A) Executive Summary
CBDCs and private digital money are increasingly converging into a coexistence debate rather than a winner-take-all contest. Public-sector projects emphasize resilience and public infrastructure roles, while private rails optimize product velocity and cross-border integration. Strategy should assume hybrid monetary plumbing with jurisdiction-specific dominance.

## C) Key Findings
- Most central banks are in research/pilot phases; only a smaller set has full retail launches [Atlantic Council CBDC Tracker, 2025] [Independent/Data] [HIGH CONFIDENCE].
- BIS projects indicate mCBDC and interoperable payment experiments are technically feasible but governance-heavy [BIS Project mBridge/Icebreaker publications, 2023-2025] [Policy/Standards] [HIGH CONFIDENCE].
- Private stablecoin transaction activity can outpace some national payment pilots, especially in crypto-native and cross-border corridors [Chainalysis reports, 2024-2025] [Independent/Industry analysis] [MEDIUM].
- Privacy architecture remains a core policy fault line; stronger privacy tends to increase AML/sanctions implementation complexity [IMF CBDC papers, 2023-2024] [Policy/Academic] [HIGH CONFIDENCE].
- [UNVERIFIED - NEEDS CONFIRMATION] Claims that CBDCs will rapidly displace bank deposits lack consistent country-level evidence. This claim requires additional verification [various policy speeches, 2024-2025] [Policy commentary] [LOW].

## D) Contradictions Found
- “CBDCs are necessary for monetary sovereignty” vs “regulated private money can achieve similar outcomes.” Core disagreement is state-control preference vs market-led innovation.
- “CBDCs improve inclusion” vs “digital-ID and device requirements can exclude vulnerable users.” Conflict depends on implementation design and offline support.

## E) Research Gaps
- - Limited publicly comparable pilot KPIs (active users, transaction quality, retention).
- - Weak evidence on long-run impact to bank funding costs in mixed CBDC/private-money environments.
